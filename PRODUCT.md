# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Leisure and business travelers aged 25–45 who expect a premium digital experience. They're browsing on desktop and mobile, often dreaming about their next trip or planning one imminently. They want to feel inspired, confident, and in control — not overwhelmed by deals and noise.

## Product Purpose

TravixGo is a premium travel platform for discovering destinations, planning itineraries, and booking flights, hotels, buses, and cars — all in one place. Success means a user arrives on the landing page, feels the quality of the brand immediately, and converts to either signing up or searching for a destination.

## Brand Personality

Confident, calm, aspirational. The brand should feel like a luxury travel concierge — knowledgeable, curated, and quietly premium. Not flashy. Not discount-oriented. Think Airbnb meets Monocle magazine.

## Anti-references

- **Expedia / Booking.com**: cluttered layouts, orange sale banners, price anchoring everywhere, 12 CTAs competing on one screen, transactional anxiety
- **Generic travel blogs**: stock-photo wallpaper heroes, lorem ipsum copy, zero brand identity
- **Dark SaaS / neon startups**: tech-forward color explosions, gradients on every text element, feels like a crypto app not a travel brand

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint over abundance** — one strong focal point per section. If everything calls for attention, nothing does.
2. **Show, don't sell** — beautiful imagery and clean typography should do the persuading. No badge spam or discount callouts that cheapen the brand.
3. **Premium through subtlety** — luxury is felt through spacing, type weight hierarchy, and soft shadows — not through gradient explosions or neon accents.
4. **Confidence in whitespace** — generous padding is not wasted space; it signals that the brand doesn't need to fight for attention.
5. **Interaction as delight** — micro-animations should feel effortless and purposeful, never decorative for their own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Keyboard navigable. All images have alt text. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Color contrast ratios must pass on both light and dark surfaces. Global audience — avoid culturally specific idioms in copy.
